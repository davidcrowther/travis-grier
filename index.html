On July 1st, 2014, new Canadian anti spam legislation that regulates digital marketing activities came into effect, changing the way that businesses can (legally) communicate with existing and potential customers via commercial electronic messages.

The anti-spam legislation, also known as CASL, is complex and brings with it stiff penalties that include fines or even jail time for failure to comply.

Discover How CASL Applies to Your Business &
What You Need to do to Comply

Who it Affects

If your business operates in Canada, or you send commercial electronic messages to Canadian customers or business partners, your activities are subject to regulation under the new anti spam law.

How to Comply

Express consent is required before a commercial electronic message can be legally sent to customers or business partners, according to CASL. How and when you must obtain consent, however, isn't straightforward.

When?

On July 1st, the first phase of CASL was implemented, bringing into effect most of the regulations pertaining to a typical business. Phases two and three, which govern rules involving computer programs and the private right of action, will be implemented on January 15, 2015 and July 1, 2017, respectively.
